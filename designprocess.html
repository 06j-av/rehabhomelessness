THE DESIGN PROCESS

This is the design cycle that was demonstrated during IDL, and will be my main reference for the explanation below.

Step 1: Define the problem

The first step in this design process was to define the problem at hand. I had never thought of an issue much at the beginning of this project, but I came up with multiple. I didn't know what to choose, so I chose to tackle homelessness, similar to a previous project that I thought that I could improve on.


Step 2: Collect information

The next step was to collect information on the issue I chose. I wanted to take mitigating homelessness to another viewpoint. We commonly think about homelessness as solely civilians who are incapable of maintaining a home (even though that is what it exactly means), I sought to touch on the other effects and causes of homelessness.

And that is where I gathered information about mental health, general physical health and substance abuse of homeless people.

Some of the statistics and research are provided here on the "Statistics" page.

Step 3: Brainstorm & analyze ideas

For this step, I have to think...

  • How could I address the issue?

  • How do I ensure that this "prototype" correlates with my research?

  • How will I present this solution?

So I got to thinking.

I have thought about this solution when I first touched homelessness for a project last year. The prototype I came across then was a homeless center that did a lot more than just sheltering the unsheltered, but it was pretty vague for a solution, so I went deeper and tried to get into more detail than last time. Therefore, I chose to conceptualize a rehabilitation center for the unsheltered.


Step 4: Develop solutions, build a model (the prototype stage)

This is where I had the most difficulties with. I created a 3D model of the concept the first time I formulated the solution for this issue, but it wasn't really sufficient in its purpose.

For this project, I had to lay out a detailed plan. What this "rehabilitation center" will have. The programs it has. How it will help the homeless reintegrate themselves with society.

  • But how will I present that?

So I have decided to code a website. Since my time working on this project was delayed quite a lot, I have a relatively short amount of time to do so. Luckily I was able to make this website, which will serve as the main presentation and prototype for showcasing my solution for the problem at hand.

My goal is to make an informative website, shining light onto this other perspective on the effects and the recovery from homelessness and highlighting my proposed mitigation strategy for this issue.

I have laid out my final plan/prototype for this issue in the "Proposed Idea" page.

Step 5: Present your ideas to others for feedback (mixed with step 6)

For this step, I gathered with a group during IDL to discuss our issues and solutions, and we gave each other feedback. When I presented my idea, I received good feedback. The feedback would, however, barely have any effect on my prototype.


The final results

My detailed solution has not yet been fully formulated since I spent most of my time trying to build the website and trying to put the plan together. The prototype plan is in the link above (the "Proposed Idea" page).
